Invasive Plants in the US: A Growing Ecological Concern¶

Invasive plant species are a threat to native biodiversity, agricultural productivity, and entire ecosystems. This dataset from the US Forest Service provides an up to date observation of plant infestation across the country.

From the perspective of Data Science we can...

  • Analyze real-world environmental data that effect our communities
  • Draw insights from structured observational records
  • Identify trends and patterns from the spread of invasive species

This analysis will provide us with the capability to be more informed about environmental concerns and how we can play our own part

Preprocessing and Cleaning¶

For readability I will make the column names lower case.

To ensure usablity I will convert Data types such as objects to datetime and specific numeric fields to float.

For simplicity I will strip all objects using dates and time to just dates.

To reduce clutter of incomplete analyses, I will drop columns missing a large amount of data.

In [34]:
# Improve Readability of Columns
df.columns = df.columns.str.strip().str.lower()

# Converting Data Types
df['last_update'] = pd.to_datetime(df['last_update'], errors='coerce')
df['date_collected'] = pd.to_datetime(df['date_collected'], errors='coerce')
df['date_collected_most_recent'] = pd.to_datetime(df['date_collected_most_recent'], errors='coerce')
df['infested_percent'] = pd.to_numeric(df['infested_percent'], errors='coerce')

# Stripping Objects of Time
df['last_update'] = df['last_update'].dt.date
df['date_collected'] = df['date_collected'].dt.date
df['date_collected_most_recent'] = df['date_collected_most_recent'].dt.date

# Dropping Columns Missing >80% of Data
threshold = 0.8
df = df.loc[:, df.isnull().mean() < threshold]

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset covers a total of 875,908 records describing invasive plant species. The table above describes the data after the proper preprocessing and cleaning to allow for a proper analysis.

Statistical Summary¶

Infestation Metrics

  • Infested Area and Total Area are both heavily right-skewed, with most values being under 1 acre, but a maximum of over 3 million acres. From this we can assume there exists a small numnber of extreme infestations that heavily influence averages.
  • Infested Percent shows a median and upper quartile of 100%. This indicates that in most cases the infestation fully occupies the affected site.

Vegetation Coverage

  • Cover Percentage(Cover PCT) has a median of 5%, ranging from 0% to 100%. This implies that many sites are sparsely vegatated, with a few that have full coverage.

Geospatial Metrics

  • GIS Acres(representing the infestation area in acres) is identical to the Total Area. As before a heavily right skew is indicated.
  • GIS Miles(representing the length of infestation features linearly). Most values are below 1 while the max is 482.78, this suggests that a few extremely long infestations exist.
  • Shape Area and Shape Len contain raw geometric data and do not provide much analytical insight.

Identifiers

  • Object ID provides identification of each record and can be disregarded for our current analytical purposes.
  • FS Unit ID indicates administrative boundaries for organization and can be helpful for grouping.

Question 2¶

What proportion of affected sites are fully infested?

As it is often very rare for anything to be 100%, fully infested will be interpreted as equal to or above a 90% threshold.

In [42]:
# All affected sites
affected_sites = df[df['infested_percent']>0]

# Fully infested sites
fully_infested_sites = affected_sites[affected_sites['infested_percent'] >= 90]

# Proportion calculation
proportion_fully_infested = round((len(fully_infested_sites) / len(affected_sites)) * 100, 2)
str(proportion_fully_infested) + '%'
Out[42]:
'76.3%'

Question 2 Conclusion¶

Using a threshold of equal to or above 90% provides us with a better idea of how many sites are overrun with invasive plant species. Given 76.3%, we can see how dire the situation is and how it is something that can not be brushed off any longer.
